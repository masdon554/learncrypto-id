a

  • Address

    • Addresses on the blockchain correspond to text strings assigned to identify a particular place or user. More specifically it is a text string that indicates the location of a particular wallet on the blockchain, which can be used to send or receive digital assets.

  • Airdrop

    • Distribution of digital assets to the public, either by holding certain other tokens or simply by virtue of being an active wallet address on a particular blockchain.

  • Algorithm

    • An unambiguous sequence of instructions used for the purpose of solving a problem.

  • Altcoin

    • Altcoins are cryptocurrencies other than Bitcoin. They share characteristics with Bitcoin but also differ in other ways. For example, some altcoins use different consensus mechanisms to generate blocks or validate transactions. Or they differentiate themselves from Bitcoin by providing new or additional capabilities, such as smart contracts or low price volatility.

  • Anonymous

    • Anonymity usually refers to a person which often means that the person's personal identity, personally identifiable information is not known. For example, in many old literary works the author cannot be traced, so the author is called "anonymous".

b

  • B-money

    • B-money was an initial proposal made by Wei Dai for an "anonymous distributed electronic cash system". Satoshi Nakamoto referenced b-money when creating Bitcoin.

  • BCH (Bitcoin Cash )

    • Bitcoin Cash is a cryptocurrency which is a fork of Bitcoin. Bitcoin Cash is a spin-off or altcoin created in 2017. In November 2018, Bitcoin Cash was further divided into two currencies, namely, Bitcoin Cash and Bitcoin SV.

  • BEP (Break-Even Point)

    • The point at which total operating costs equal current value or revenue.

  • BEP-2

    • A technical standard that defines a set of rules for the issuance and management of tokens in the Binance Chain ecosystem.

  • BEP-20

    • BEP-20 is a token standard on the Binance Smart Chain that extends ERC-20, the most common Ethereum token standard. You can think of it as a blueprint for tokens that dictates how they can be spent, who can spend them, and other rules for their use. Due to its similarity to Binance Chain's BEP-2 and Ethereum's ERC-20, it is compatible with both.

  • BitGold

    • Bit gold was one of the earliest attempts to create a decentralized virtual currency, proposed by blockchain pioneer Nick Szabo in 1998.

  • Bitcoin Core

    • Leading implementation of software that allows users to interact with the Bitcoin network. Originally released by Satoshi

  • Bitcoin Halving

    • Bitcoin Halving is a cycle every 4 years, when rewards for bitcoin mining transactions are cut in half. This is used to create a decreasing issuance rate to arrive at an ultimately limited supply of the crypto asset. This event also cut Bitcoin's inflation rate in half and the rate at which new bitcoins entered circulation. The last Bitcoin Halving occurred on May 11, 2020, around 3pm EST, resulting in a block reward of 6.25 BTC. This system will continue until around 2140.

  • Blind Signature

    • In cryptography a blind signature, as introduced by David Chaum, is a form of digital signature in which the contents of the message are disguised before being signed. The resulting blind signature can be publicly verified against the original message that is not obfuscated by normal digital signature means.

  • Block Explorer

    • An online blockchain web page that allows users to browse information about blocks, transactions, balances and transaction history.

  • Block Header

    • The part of the block that contains metadata and a summary of block transactions. This is the information that is hashed when mining.

  • Block Height

    • The number of blocks in the chain between itself and the first block on that blockchain (genesis block or block 0)

  • Block Reward

    • The number of coins awarded by the blockchain protocol to cryptocurrency miners for each successfully mined and validated block.

  • BNB (Binance )

    • Binance is a cryptocurrency created by Changpeng Zhao as founder and CEO. This coin is divided into 2 types, namely Binance Coin which was launched in 2017 and Binance Smart Chain in 2020.

  • Bounty

    • Rewards posted by groups or individuals to encourage specific work, behavior (such as referrals), or development.

  • BTC (Bitcoin )

    • Bitcoin is the first decentralized cryptocurrency. This coin was created by an anonymous individual/group known as Satoshi Nakamoto.

c

  • Centralize

    • When planning and decision-making mechanisms are concentrated at a certain point in a system.

  • Central Bank

    • A financial institution that acts as a monetary authority and manages a country's currency, interest rates, and money supply.

  • Circulating Supply (Pasokan yang beredar)

    • An estimate of the number of best cryptocurrency coins or tokens available to the public and circulating on the market.

  • Cloud

    • In computer science a cloud is a collection of shared resources, available to many users via the internet.

  • Coin Crypto

    • Cryptocurrency that is independent from other platforms, which is used as an exchange of value.

  • Coin Meme

    • meme coins are inspired by jokes or silly views about other well-known cryptocurrencies. They usually gain popularity in a short period of time, often hyped online by prominent crypto influencers and retail investors trying to exploit short-term gains.

  • Confirmation Time (Waktu konfirmasi)

    • The time that elapses when a transaction is sent to the network and that time is recorded in the confirmed block.

  • CPU (Central Processing Unit)

    • The part of a computer that is responsible for interpreting computer program instructions and carrying out operations.

  • Cryptocurrency

    • A digital currency secured with cryptography to function as a medium of exchange in a peer-to-peer (P2P) economic system.

  • Cyberbucks

    • A term from the Digicash system. This means banks and the government cannot track payers in two-party transactions. ... DigiCash uses a digital currency called "cyberbucks".

    d

  • DAC (Decentralized Autonomous Cooperative)

    • An organization controlled by shareholders rather than a central authority.

  • DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization)

    • A system of hard-coded rules that determines which actions a decentralized organization will take.

    • Dapps (Decentralized Applications)

      • Applications built on a decentralized network that combines smart contracts and a front-end user interface. On Ethereum, smart contracts are accessible and transparent – like an open API – so your dapp can even include smart contracts that other people have written.

    • David Chaum

      • David Chaum is an American computer scientist and cryptographer. He is known as a pioneer in cryptography and privacy-preserving technologies, and is widely recognized as the inventor of digital money.

    • Desentralisasi

      • Is the process in which organizational activities, especially those related to planning and decision making, are distributed or delegated away from authorized locations or groups.

    • (Decentralized Exchange)

      • Exchanges that do not require users to deposit funds to initiate trading and do not hold funds for users. Instead, users trade directly from their own wallets.

    • DeFi (Decentralized Finance)

      • The ecosystem consists of decentralized financial applications developed on top of the blockchain network.

    • Description

      • The action restores the encryption process so that unreadable data (ciphertext) can be converted into readable data (plaintext).

    • Difficulty (Difficulty)

      • In cryptocurrency, Difficulty indicates the difficulty required to mine a block.

    • Digicash

      • DigiCash is an implementation of e-cash which is an electronic money company founded by David Chaum in 1989.

    • Double Spending

      • An event when a certain amount of coins is spent more than once. Usually caused by a race attack or 51% attack.

    e

    • ETH (Ethereum )

      • Ethereum is a technology that hosts digital money, global payments and applications. The community has built a thriving digital economy, bold new ways for content creators to make money online, and more. It's open to everyone, wherever you are in the world – all you need is the internet.

    • E-cash (Electronic Cash)

      • Is the initial concept of digital currency that exists today. Created by David Chaum who is an American computer scientist and cryptographer.

    • Encryption

      • Convert information or data into secure code to prevent open standards-based architecture and specifications to accelerate Enterprise Ethereum adoption.

    • ERC-20

      • Technical standards used to issue and implement tokens on the Ethereum blockchain proposed in November 2015 by Fabian Vogelsteller.

    • Exchange

      • A marketplace for cryptocurrencies where users can buy and sell coins or tokens.

    f

    • FATF (Financial Action Task Force)

      • Translated from English, the Financial Action Task Force, also known by its French name, Groupe d'action financière, is an intergovernmental organization founded in 1989 on the initiative of the G7 to develop policies to combat money laundering. In 2001, its mandate was expanded to include terrorism financing.

    • Fiat

      • Money that has been declared by the government as legal tender.

    • Full Node

      • A computer that fully implements the entire rules of the underlying blockchain network and fully validates transactions and blocks on the blockchain.

    g

    • Gas

      • A pricing mechanism used on the Ethereum blockchain to calculate smart contract operating costs and transaction fees.

    • Genesis Block

      • The first block recorded in the respective blockchain network, is also referred to as block 0 or block 1.

    • Gwei

      • A small denomination of Ether. It is widely used as a measure of gas prices. 1,000,000,000 wei = 1 Giga Wei (Gwei).

    h

    • Hash

      • The output produced by a hash function after a piece of data is mapped. Can also be called a hash value, hash code, or digest.

    • Hashcash

      • Hashcash is a proof-of-work system used to limit email spam and denial of service attacks, and has recently become known for its use in bitcoin (and other cryptocurrencies) as part of a mining algorithm. Hashcash was proposed in 1997 by Adam Back and described more formally in Back's 2002 paper "Hashcash – A Denial of Service Counter-Measure".

    • Hash Rate

      • The speed at which a computer or mining hardware can calculate a new hash. Usually measured in hashes per second(h/s).

    • HODL

      • An incorrect typing of Hold came from BitcoinTalk which was also installed as an acronym for Hold on for Dear Life - to maintain ownership of the coin and not sell it.

    i

    • ICO (Initial Coin Offering)

      • A fundraising method where new projects will sell their cryptocurrency to investors.

    • IEO (Initial Exchange Offering)

      • A fundraising method designed to reduce risks for token buyers by introducing a trusted intermediary between the project team and users.

    • IOU (I Owe You)

      • An acronym that stands for "I owe you" and refers to an informal document that acknowledges one party's debt to another party.

    j

    k

    • KYC (Know Your Customer)

      • A standard procedure in the financial industry that allows companies to identify their customers and comply with AML KYC laws.

    l

    • Ledger

      • A physical book or digital computer file in which monetary and financial transactions are tracked and recorded.

    • Liquidity

      • The ability to sell or buy certain assets without causing significant market price fluctuations for those assets.

    • LTC (Litecoin )

      • Litecoin is a cryptocurrency created in 2011 and open source software under the MIT/X11 license. Litecoin is one of the cryptocurrencies derived from Bitcoin Core QT, which means everything in Litecoin is not much different from Bitcoin.

    m

    • Mainnet

      • Fully developed and deployed blockchain protocol where transactions are broadcast, verified, and recorded.

    • Margin Trading

      • Trading using borrowed funds - note: this is a high-risk strategy and should only be undertaken by experienced investors.

    • Market Capitalization

      • Total trading value of a given coin - calculated by the product of the coin supply at the current price.

    • Masternode

      • Nodes on a network often require a minimum amount of certain coins staked to access staking rewards.

    • Markle Tree

      • How to organize and organize large amounts of data so that it is easier to process. Hash-based data structure.

    • Metadata

      • Data that includes information about other data, such as information about certain transaction features.

    • Mining

      • Verification of transactions on a blockchain network, where transactions are added as entries to the blockchain ledger.

    • Moon

      • A colloquial expression used to describe a cryptocurrency or other asset that is experiencing a strong upward market trend.

    • Multisignature

      • A wallet that requires another party to authorize transactions before they are broadcast to the network.

    n

    • NFT (Non-Fungible Token)

      • NFT is a unique and non-fungible unit of data stored on a digital ledger (Blockchain). NFTs can be used to represent easily reproduced items such as photos, videos, audio, and other types of digital files as unique items (analogous to a certificate of authenticity), and use blockchain technology to create verified and public proof of ownership.

    • Nodes

      • A participant in a blockchain network who communicates with other participants to ensure the security and integrity of the system.

    • Nonce

      • A one-time arbitrary string or number created for verification purposes to prevent repetition of previous transactions.

    o

    • Off-Chain

      • Transactions that occur from a particular blockchain network, which can then be reported or aggregated together before being submitted to the main chain.

    • Oracle

      • A data source or feed from a third party used to determine outcomes for a smart contract.

    p

    • P2P (Peer to Peer)

      • When two or more computers are connected and share workloads or resources without relying on a centralized server.

    • Paper Wallet

      • A sheet of paper on which a cryptocurrency address and corresponding private key are physically printed.

    • Phishing

      • A malicious attack in which a malicious actor will attempt to obtain a user's credentials to gain unauthorized access to their account.

    • Ponzi Scheme

      • A scam in which new investors' funds are used to pay promised returns to previous investors. Don't invest in this.

    • Private Key (Privacy Key)

      • In cryptocurrency, a private key is a long number that allows a user to sign a transaction and generate a recipient address.

    • PoS (Proof Of Stake)

      • A consensus mechanism that rewards block validators according to the number of coins they stake.

    q

    • Quantum Computing

      • Quantum computing works using particles that can be in superposition. These particles represent qubits instead of bits and can take the values 1, 0 or both simultaneously.

    r

    • Race Attack

      • When two transactions are made with the same funds at the same time, with the intention of spending those funds twice.

    • Resistance

      • A term in Technical Analysis (TA). When rising prices encounter resistance. Usually compared to the previous highest price.

    • ROI (Return Of Investment)

      • A measure used to assess the efficiency of an investment. The ratio between net profit and net costs.

    s

    • Smart Contract/Smart Contact

      • Smart contract is a term used to describe a computer protocol that facilitates a contract or agreement between one party and another without going through a third party.

    • Satoshi

      • The smallest unit of bitcoin, as defined by the Bitcoin protocol. It is equal to one hundred millionth of a bitcoin or 0.00000001 BTC.

    • Satoshi Nakamoto

      • The pseudonym of the creator or creator of the bitcoin protocol and whitepaper.

    • Seed Phrase

      • Seed phrase or menmonic seed is a collection of words that can be used to access your cryptocurrency wallet.

    • SegWit

      • A process where the transaction signature is separated from the bitcoin transaction. Allows more transactions to fit in one block.

    • Smart Contract (Smart Contract)

      • Automatic contracts that trigger specific actions when predefined conditions are met. This works like an 'If...then' condition as for example, it is necessary that condition C is met before money can be transferred from A to B.

    • Source Code (Source Code)

      • Computer code, which is responsible for determining how the software will function based on a list of instructions and statements.

    • Stablecoins

      • A type of cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value, rather than experiencing significant price changes. Such as USDT, USDC, BUSD, DAI, HUSD.

    t

    • Ticker

      • The trading 'symbol' or short name (usually in capital letters) that refers to the coin on the trading platform. Example: BNB

    • Token

      • Tokens (not to be confused with coins) are digital units issued on a blockchain. They can store value or be redeemed for assets.

    • Token Lookup

      • Token lockup or vesting period refers to the time span during which tokens or coins are not permitted to be transferred or traded.

    • Token Sale

      • Issuance of tokens in exchange for other cryptocurrencies. Also called an Initial Coin Offering.

    • Token Supply

      • Refers to the number of coins or tokens that currently exist and are in circulation or locked somehow.

    • TXID (Transaction ID)

      • A transaction ID (TXID) is a unique character string that labels each transaction on the blockchain.

    u

    • Units Of Account

      • One of the main properties of money. Allows measurement and comparison of the value of different things.

    • UTXO

      • Output created in a transaction, which must be referenced in future transactions to spend funds.

    • UI (User Interface)

      • The interface where interaction between humans and machines occurs. It defines how the user can interact with the machine.

    v

    • Verification Code

      • A code is sent to a second device to confirm the identity of the person logging into the account. Used for Two Factor Authentication.

    • Virtual Machine

      • An emulated computer system, or a distributed system designed to replicate computer architectural features.

    • Volatility

      • How quickly and how much the price of an asset changes. Calculated in terms of the standard deviation in the annual return of an asset over a certain time period.

    • Volume

      • A measurement of the number of individual units of an asset that change hands in the market during a given time.

    w

    • Wallets

      • Used to send and receive cryptocurrency. Different types include software wallets, hardware wallets, and paper wallets.

    • Wei

      • The smallest possible denomination of ether (ETH), the currency used on the Ethereum network. Often used when referring to gas prices.

    • Whitelist

      • A list of authorized or trusted individuals, computer programs, or cryptocurrency addresses in connection with a service or event.
